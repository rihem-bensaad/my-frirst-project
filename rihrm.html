      Djerba is an island on the Tunisian coast known for its Mediterranean beaches and its white desert cities influenced by Berber, Arab, Jewish and African cultures. Its main city is Houmt Souk. It is renowned for its craft markets, its fishing port and its 16th century fortress, Borj el Kebir. To the south is the El Ghriba synagogue, a place of pilgrimage for the Jews of North Africa.
